What patients tell us

The Problem

Your primary care office is here. Your rheumatologist is forty minutes away in King of Prussia. The pulmonologist is at a hospital network that uses a different chart. Nobody talks to anybody else, and you become the person carrying the printouts.

It is tiring. Most people stop scheduling the appointments they need.

A system patients have to fight against.

How we work

The PMSI Way

Same group, same chart. Your family doctor at Brookside can read the note your PMSI neurologist wrote last Tuesday. The referral does not get faxed, lost or repeated. You drive twelve minutes instead of an hour.

That is the practical version of "coordinated care."

Healthcare 101

The real cost of fragmented care.

When your primary care doctor, your specialists and your pharmacy do not share a chart, the work of keeping everyone informed quietly becomes your job. You repeat your history at every visit. You carry the printouts. You try to remember what dose got changed last spring.

Care that is actually coordinated is not a perk. It is the practical reason a chronic condition gets caught at a well-visit instead of in an ER three years later. It is one of the few things in modern healthcare that genuinely makes a daily difference.

Common questions, answered

How do I become a patient at PMSI?
Call the office closest to you. Most of our family practice locations can fit new patients in within a week.
What insurance do you accept?
We take most major plans, though coverage can differ from one specialty to another. The current list is on our patient resources page, and the front desk will check yours when you call.
Can I see specialists without a referral?
A lot of our specialty divisions will book directly, but your insurance plan may still need a referral on file for it to be covered. If you are not sure, call us first and we will walk you through it.
How do I access my records?
FollowMyHealth is our patient portal. You can pull up lab results, request refills, message the office, and look at your history without picking up the phone.
